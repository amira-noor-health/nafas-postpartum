# NAFAS demo video — 2:45 master script

Record vertically or in a clean mobile-sized browser view. Use English narration and captions. Do not use copyrighted music or real patient information.

## 0:00–0:15 — Hook

**On screen:** NAFAS home, then the three demo scenarios.

**Narration:**

“After birth, a mother may say only, ‘I’m tired.’ But underneath that sentence may be pain, fragmented sleep, feeding demands, low nutrition, emotional distress, and too little practical support. NAFAS helps turn that unfiltered story into shared action.”

## 0:15–0:30 — The product promise

**On screen:** Select **EN • JUDGE MODE**. Briefly show the five-step pipeline.

**Narration:**

“NAFAS is an Arabic-first, bilingual postpartum recovery companion. One voice or text check-in becomes a safety priority, a six-dimension recovery map, a focused 24-hour plan, and one clear task for her care circle.”

## 0:30–1:05 — Live same-day scenario

**On screen:** Choose **Same-day review**. Let the loading stages appear. Keep a small ‘live GPT-5.6 response’ caption on screen.

**Narration:**

“I’ll use a fictional, de-identified case. Before GPT is called, NAFAS rejects direct identifiers and runs an independent deterministic red-flag check. This case contains worsening wound pain and wound discharge, so the urgent priority is preserved. GPT-5.6 Terra then returns strict structured output in the selected language.”

## 1:05–1:35 — Recovery map and 24-hour plan

**On screen:** Scroll through the safety card, radar map, six dimensions, and four plan steps.

**Narration:**

“Instead of another long answer, NAFAS shows the whole recovery load. The plan contains no more than four actions, and every action has a time and an owner. This reduces decisions for the mother while keeping missing safety information visible.”

## 1:35–1:58 — Care-circle handoff

**On screen:** Tap **Preview caregiver view**, show the task, then tap **I’ve got this task**.

**Narration:**

“Support becomes one observable action. The care-circle view does not add advice. It transfers a real piece of work away from the mother and creates a protected recovery window.”

## 1:58–2:15 — Clinician-ready summary

**On screen:** Open the clinician summary and show the three clearly separated sections.

**Narration:**

“For healthcare communication, NAFAS preserves what the mother reported, separates organized concerns, and labels what is still unknown. It does not diagnose or invent reassuring findings.”

## 2:15–2:32 — Emergency bypass

**On screen:** Return home and select **Immediate action**. Show the emergency result and the ‘no model call’ execution badge.

**Narration:**

“In the emergency demo, the deterministic safety path bypasses the model completely and returns immediate human-help steps. GPT is not the source of truth for this boundary.”

## 2:32–2:45 — Codex and close

**On screen:** Show the pipeline, a quick code/test montage, then the NAFAS logo and live URL.

**Narration:**

“I am a clinical pharmacist, maternal-health creator, and non-technical founder. Codex helped turn my clinical framing into a bilingual, tested, public product during Build Week. NAFAS: postpartum recovery, shared.”

## Recording checklist

- Keep the final video under 3:00.
- Use a fictional case only.
- Show the live website URL once.
- Show Judge Mode, a live GPT response, the caregiver view, clinician summary, and emergency bypass.
- Include English captions even if the spoken narration is English.
- Upload publicly to YouTube and test playback while signed out.
- Do not include copyrighted music or third-party trademarks.
